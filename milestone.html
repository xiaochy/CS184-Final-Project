Milestone Status Report

Claire Fang, Chuyang Xiao, Yifan Yin, Xiaoru Zhao

Our Presentation Slides

Summary:

Based on the original snow simulation paper, we finished all steps of the MPM method: rasterize the particle data to the grid, compute particle volumes and densities, compute grid forces, update velocities on grid, handle the grid-based collisions with the environment (i.e. the walls), update deformation gradient, update particle velocities, handle particle-based collision with the environment, and update particle positions. For simplicity, we ignored the implicit time-integration of velocities. We plan to add the implicit velocities in the future weeks.

Preliminary Results

  • Implemented our own data structure, including Grid, GridNode, Particle, SnowParticleMaterial, and shape.
  • Implemented full material point method to simulate snow dynamics, including collision with grid and object.
  • Achieved visualization simulating, simulating a snow ball exploding.

Reflection On Progress

We succeeded in implementing the baseline model. During the process, we ignored the implicit velocity update for simplicity and we plan to go back to it after we finish all implementation. We also plan to be able to create and combine multiple shapes of snow particle clusters in the future. Our goal was to be able to simulate easy snow behaviors by the end of the first stage.
